Welcome to this info-page about the Icehotel



In 1989 a Swedish man named Yngve Bergqvist made a 250 squaremeter big igloo in Jukkasjärvi, Sweden, next to Torneälv. This igloo was an art gallery made of ice and snow.


In an interview with Yngve he tells:


On a cold winter day all our warm cabins were fully booked for the night but we had a group who wanted to sleep over. I suggested that the guests could sleep in the cold Artic Hall. To my surprise, the guests accepted and were provided with warm sleeping bags and given instructions on how to sleep in the cold. The next morning they were fascinated by the experience. Icehotel was born



Ever since its inception in 1989, Icehotel has been an art exhibition; ephemeral and entirely focused on the idea of exploring art by presenting creative individuals to ice and snow. All art on Icehotel is original work and is trademarked under the Trademark Act.

It's not about coming up with this year's theme or hiring the most skilled ice sculptors. It is collecting the most innovative and ground-breaking ideas from all corners of the world, bringing them to Jukkasjärvi and, by providing materials, know-how and tools, transforming these ideas into hotel suites of ice and snow.
